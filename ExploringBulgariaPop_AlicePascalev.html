A Dying Country? Exploring Bulgaria's Population Decline

In the past two decades alone, Bulgaria's population has decreased by over 20%, from the modest figure of just over 8 million, to under 6.5 million in 2022.

Source: Eurostat

This is part of a larger trend. During the peak of the Communist Party in the 1960s and 1970s, the birth rate was at least 50% higher than the death rate, some years double or more. The fall of Communism in 1989 was followed by a time of economic uncertainty, high emigration, and failing goverment services. This resulted in low birth rates, and higher death rates as an aging population faces issues receiving care in the weak, understaffed healthcare system.1

Despite becoming a European Union member state in 2007, Bulgaria still had one of the lowest GDPs per capita in Europe, and the lowest in the European Union - and this trend is projected to continue years into the future.

With EU membership, comes EU passports. An increasing number of Bulgarians are taking advantage of their EU citizenships to emigrate to other member states, resulting in brain drain2. Bulgaria faces an uncertain future as its population continues to decline, influenced by a combination of a low birth rate, high death rate, and the availability of better economic opportunities in nearby countries, facilitated by the ease of immigration with EU citizenship.
